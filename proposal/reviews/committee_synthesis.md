# Committee Synthesis

## Top weaknesses

1. **The proposal overclaims relative to local evidence.**
   The current draft turns method-specific observations into field-wide conclusions, especially around the absence of a "context compiler" and the form of the internal adaptive state.

2. **The core hypothesis is not operational enough.**
   Pillar I states a typed state decomposition as if it were already justified, while the local mechanistic evidence only supports narrower candidate latent quantities.

3. **The method section lacks measurable acceptance criteria.**
   Pillar II needs explicit objectives, baselines, and tests for retention, invalidation, and usefulness of compiled memory.

4. **The validation scope is too broad for one executable program.**
   Pillar III currently mixes offline shift, calibration, abstention, embodied adaptation, and multimodal evaluation without a staged fallback path.

5. **The timeline assumes scale before proving the mechanism.**
   The current schedule risks committing to AdA/ReLIC/LMAct-style infrastructure before the proposal demonstrates value in controlled settings.

## Patch strategy

- Reframe the proposal around one primary claim: explicit candidate algorithmic state can be tested and exploited better than opaque history-conditioned state.
- Treat experience compilation as the architectural consequence of Pillar I rather than a standalone grand claim.
- Make embodied and multimodal evaluation a late-stage bounded validation thread gated on earlier milestones.
- Add concrete baselines, evaluation metrics, and fallback plans.
